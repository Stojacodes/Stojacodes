I’m interested in how AI can enhance human capacities—not only by automating tasks, but by helping people learn, think, decide and act with greater agency.

My work follows that question from individual understanding to collective judgment. I design and build working prototypes to explore where AI can genuinely extend human capability, and where it needs to remain bounded by human values and oversight.

## The thread through my work

### Learnscape — AI for learning

Learnscape explored whether generative AI could turn curiosity into a structured route through knowledge. Starting from a topic or question, the prototype generated an accessible video course designed to help someone enter an unfamiliar subject and develop their understanding.

The project began with a simple question: **can AI make high-quality, self-directed learning easier to begin and sustain?**

### Deliberate — AI for judgment and coordination

Deliberate extends that enquiry from learning alone to reasoning and acting together. It is an independent research project exploring how deliberation and agentic AI can be combined without asking people to give up their judgment.

The current experiments investigate how people can articulate goals, surface disagreement and form shared mandates before delegating work to AI agents. The aim is not simply more capable automation, but warranted autonomy: systems that remain faithful to the people they act for and strengthen, rather than displace, human understanding.

Learnscape asked how AI might help a person navigate knowledge. Deliberate asks how it might help people navigate one another—and then coordinate agents around what they actually mean.

## What I’m exploring now

- Deliberation as an interface for human–AI coordination
- Shared mandates, value alignment and trustworthy delegation
- AI systems that increase understanding rather than conceal complexity
- Product and research methods for testing emerging forms of human agency
